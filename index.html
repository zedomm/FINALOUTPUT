EASTWOOD CITY

Birth and boom of the IT BPO in the Philippines

Introduction:

Eastwood City, located in Libis, Quezon City, stands as one of the Philippines’ first and most successful Information Technology (IT) zones. Developed by Megaworld Corporation, it has become a symbol of progress and modern urban living, blending business, leisure, and residential spaces in one integrated township. We chose Eastwood City because it represents how the Philippines has embraced digital transformation and innovation while fostering community and culture. It is not only a center for technology and commerce but also a place where people gather, celebrate, and experience a sense of belonging.

Chronological Historical Development

Eastwood City began in 1997, when Megaworld Corporation envisioned a “city within a city,” a township where people could live, work, and play in one area. In 1999, the Philippine Economic Zone Authority (PEZA) officially recognized Eastwood as the country’s first IT park, making it a prime location for global IT and BPO (Business Process Outsourcing) companies. During the 2000s, it became one of the main hubs for international outsourcing firms, helping boost the Philippine economy and creating thousands of job opportunities for Filipinos. Over time, Eastwood also grew into a cultural and lifestyle center, with malls, restaurants, and public spaces hosting community events and celebrations. In 2024, as it marked its 28th anniversary, Megaworld announced a ₱25-billion redevelopment plan to modernize Eastwood City. Enhancing sustainability, design, and digital infrastructure while preserving its identity as a pioneer IT hub.

Cultural and Historical Significance

Eastwood City’s historical importance lies in being a pioneer of digital progress in the Philippines. It played a crucial role in positioning the country as one of the world’s leading IT-BPO destinations. Beyond its economic role, Eastwood also became a cultural landmark, hosting events like the annual New Year’s Eve Countdown, art fairs, concerts, and pet-friendly festivals that bring people from different walks of life together. It represents the evolving Filipino identity—modern yet community-oriented, tech-driven yet culturally grounded. Eastwood has become a living example of how urban development can merge technology, business, and lifestyle while still reflecting Filipino values of connection and celebration.

Conclusion and Reflection

Eastwood City IT Zone stands as a testament to the Philippines’ growth in the digital era. From a simple vision in the late 1990s, it has grown into a hub of innovation, creativity, and community. Reflecting on Eastwood’s story made us realize how progress and culture can coexist when development is guided by vision and purpose. As future IT professionals, we are inspired by Eastwood to see technology not just as a tool for advancement but also as a bridge for people: to connect, create, and build a better future together.
